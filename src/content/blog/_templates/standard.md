REPLACE this lede with one paragraph that earns the click: state the question, hint at the answer, and signal that the reader is in the right place. Keep it tight — three to five sentences.

## The first idea

Open each section with a single concrete sentence. Then expand. Then land it. Avoid throat-clearing transitions ("In this article, we'll cover…"); the headings already announce the structure.

When you use a number, anchor it: where it came from, what it assumes, and the kind of error it's prone to.

> Use blockquotes sparingly. They work best for a single sharp sentence the reader should remember.

## The second idea

If a sub-point really needs its own heading, use `###`. Otherwise just keep writing — extra headings fragment the read.

- Bullet lists are for parallel items, not for breaking up a single thought.
- If the items aren't parallel, write a paragraph.
- If the items need explanation, use a `**Bold lead-in:**` paragraph instead.

## The honest caveat

Every finance article hides assumptions. Surface them. What did you not model? What changes the conclusion? The reader trusts you more, not less, when you admit what's uncertain.

## What to do with this

End with a small, concrete action — not "talk to a financial advisor," but something the reader can do this week. Then link to the calculator or to a related post that helps them do it.
