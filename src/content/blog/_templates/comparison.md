REPLACE this lede with the punch-line. Most readers want the answer in the first paragraph, and then proof. Give them both: state who wins under typical conditions, and what makes the answer flip.

## What each one actually is

Define **A** and **B** in one sentence each. No history detours, no founder anecdotes. Save those for a dedicated post.

## When A wins

One or two paragraphs. A realistic scenario, not a stylized one.

## When B wins

Same shape. A reader should finish this section knowing how to recognize which side of the line they're on.

## The honest tie

Most "A vs B" questions have a range where the choice doesn't really matter. Say so. It builds trust.
