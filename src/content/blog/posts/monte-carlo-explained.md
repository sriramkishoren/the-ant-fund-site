When a planner shows you "your portfolio will average 7% per year," they are answering the wrong question. The question that actually matters is: across the thousands of plausible futures, how often does the plan *run out of money*?

Monte Carlo simulation answers that by running the plan against ten thousand randomly-generated return paths, then counting how many of them ended above zero.
