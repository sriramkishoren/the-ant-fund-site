The 4% rule started life as a research finding from a 1994 paper by William Bengen. It said: if you retire with a stock-and-bond portfolio and pull 4% of the starting value every year — adjusted up for inflation — the money has historically lasted at least thirty years in every back-tested case.

That's a useful starting point. It's not a guarantee.
